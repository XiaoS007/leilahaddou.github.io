Investigations


Most examples of my work come from my time with the Guardian and all images and stories are copyright to them unless otherwise stated.


• Inmate suicide figures expose the human toll of the prison crisis


For the first time, the Guardian looked at the names and stories of those who had tragically taken their own lives in prison, following a 64% rise in suicides in only a year. We found shocking stories of young men sent to jail due to lack of mental health spaces, continual failures to spot the warning signs against the backdrop of cuts to facilities, funding and staff cuts. 26% of the prisoners were on remand and had not yet been convicted of any crime.


Offshore Tax:

A short series of stories resulting from a data leak to the ICIJ of offshore accounts held with a Jersey bank. Researching the story on such a messy and large dataset required the use of web-scraping and cross-matching information for a more complete analysis.


• Top Tory has family link with offshore banker who gave the party £800,000


The second set of stories from the offshore banking leaks focused on the celebrities and sports personalities found to hold accounts in Jersey.


• Leak reveals Nando's link to offshore tax havens


The final instalment in the offshore tax series revealed the intricate web of Nando's money through 14 jurisdictions before ultimately ending up in a trust fund designed to avoid inheritance tax. Other stories in the paper also focussed on other tycoons found to be included in the leaked Jersey files.

• Tesco owns enough land to fit 15,000 homes

An original deep-dive data investigation into the land holdings of supermarket chain Tesco, who had previously faced criticism from the Competition Commission on its land banking activities. The analysis involved thousands of land registry titles to determine the extent of the unused sites owned by the company. They have since announced that excess land will be sold to house builders.


• Revealed: Thousands of Britons on benefits across EU


An investigation into the number of British people claiming unemployment benefits abroad, with data obtained from the relevant offices in each EU member country.

• 'Independent' court scrutinising MI5 is located inside the Home Office

A joint investigation with Ian Cobain examining the location of the most secretive court in the country, the Investigatory Powers Tribunal. As the body responsible for the oversight of surveillance activities regulated by RIPA, it has to date, upheld only 10 complaints out of 1500 and none against the intelligence services. This investigation questions its independence, given its funding by and location within the Home Office.
