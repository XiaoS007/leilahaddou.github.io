Data Journalism

I worked with the Datablog on a four month placement in 2014. Below are some examples of quick turnaround data stories using national and international statistics, both to support the news desk or as standalone pieces.

Maternal mortality down 45%- but 33 women an hour are still dying

A report by the World Health Organisation shows significant decrease in pregnancy and childbirth related deaths since 1990

• Prison system cuts point to further cheap super-prisons

Further £149m of savings will see public jails encouraged to match the expenditure of privately-run super-prisons

• Domestic workers around the world- the big picture

There are an estimated 52 million domestic workers worldwide, but the kafala system in the Middle East can make life particularly hard there

• London welcomes largest number of visitors since 1961

In 2013, the number of visitors to the UK increased by 5.6% on 2012, with the 16.8m people visiting the capital making it the best year for London tourism since the early 1960s

• New cars meet EU emissions targets two years early

Provisional data shows new cars sold in 2013 were 4% more efficient than the year before.

• The Future of Scotland's currency  continues to divide opinion, polls show

YouGov poll finds 53% of Britons outside Scotland oppose the country keeping the pound and believe it has no viable alternative to sterling should it vote for independence


• Death penalty statistics- country by country

At least 778 executions were carried out across the world in 2013, according to figures out today from Amnesty International. See how the data breaks down by country

• Press freedom 2014- the global picture

Press freedom around the world has declined to its lowest level in over a decade, with major setbacks in several Middle Eastern states as well as Turkey and Ukraine
